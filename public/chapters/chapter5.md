    <p>

<h2>Key Policy Recommendations</h2>

<div class="policy-grid">

  <div class="bubble">
    <span class="icon">üí≥</span>
    <h3>Reduce Costs for Small Merchants</h3>
    <p>
      Transaction costs are one of the strongest barriers to digital payment adoption,
      particularly for small businesses.
    </p>
    <ul>
      <li>Fee caps for small-value digital transactions</li>
      <li>Temporary subsidies for payment terminals</li>
      <li>Reduced interchange fees for small merchants</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="bubble">
    <span class="icon">üîó</span>
    <h3>Support Low-Cost, Interoperable Payments</h3>
    <p>
      QR-code payments and account-to-account transfers reduce infrastructure requirements
      and ongoing fees. Initiatives like the European Payments Initiative (EPI) support
      European solutions.
    </p>
    <ul>
      <li>Lower merchant costs</li>
      <li>Reduced market fragmentation</li>
      <li>Increased competition and innovation</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="bubble">
    <span class="icon">üéØ</span>
    <h3>Use Voluntary Incentives Instead of Mandates</h3>
    <p>
      In a country with strong privacy expectations and regulatory safeguards,
      incentive-based approaches are more likely to be perceived as legitimate and fair.
    </p>
    <ul>
      <li>Tax credits for digital payment investments</li>
      <li>Investment allowances for payment infrastructure</li>
      <li>Support programs for small and family-run businesses</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="bubble">
    <span class="icon">üìä</span>
    <h3>Improve Transparency and Merchant Support</h3>
    <p>
      While card fees are visible, cash handling also involves costs related to time,
      security, transport, and accounting.
    </p>
    <ul>
      <li>Cost-comparison tools and calculators</li>
      <li>Workshops via chambers of commerce</li>
      <li>Clear, accessible information on payment options</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="bubble">
    <span class="icon">üîê</span>
    <h3>Design for Trust and Privacy</h3>
    <p>
      Privacy concerns remain a key reason for cash preference.
      Privacy-preserving digital payment solutions with minimal data collection
      and strong security standards can help build trust.
    </p>
  </div>

<h2>Best Practices in the Global South</h2>

<div class="footer-bubble">
  <p>
    Germany‚Äôs experience offers lessons for technology and development more broadly.
    In many countries in the Global South, mobile money and QR-based systems
    (such as M-Pesa in Kenya) have expanded financial inclusion by combining
    low costs, ease of use, and strong local trust.
  </p>
  <p>
    International initiatives such as the <strong>Better Than Cash Alliance</strong>
    demonstrate that successful adoption depends less on technology alone and more on:
  </p>
  <ul>
    <li>Supportive policy frameworks</li>
    <li>Ecosystem partnerships</li>
    <li>Respect for local practices and user agency</li>
  </ul>
</div>
